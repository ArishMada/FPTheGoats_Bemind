Escapism

What do we mean by escapism?

Escapism is the avoidance of reality by absorption of the mind in entertainment. In other words, it would be defined as the tendency to seek distraction fro unpleasant realities. It has been proven to be a good of way managing stress by offering a way to take the pressure off and temporarily disconnect.It can provide enough space and distance to enjoy the present moment, a welcome relief when life’s getting on top of you. Escapism can form from reading a book to traveling to a new place, but the one we propose is a relaxation with breathing exercises and music to calm your mind. Our goal is to make you feel free and ready to face any obstacle you may be facing. Our imaginations can be a wonderful thing for alleviating stress. It offers us time and space to better deal with our problems. No one understands your mental health better than you, with some time with yourself you might find a way to make your life more enjoyable.

Benefits

  • Relieve pressure
  • Take a step back from a stressful day
  • Free your mind
  • Breath out all the stress, anger and pain that you are carrying on you back
  • Recharge your physical and mental batteries
  • Snap out of your emotions when you feel overwhelmed

While escapism might not be the solution to life's big problems, it might give you the step you need to look at your problem from a different approach. It will surely make you more positive.

Explanation

This Session is the first you have to do in a day and it is pretty effective to prepare you for the daily stress. The morning is one of the most important moments in the day because your attitude and energy at that time will determine how your day will be, as the saying "get up on the wrong side of the bed" highlights it, your morning routing will determine how you will feel for the rest of the day.This session is supposed to be about 10 min but of course you can do it for how long as you want, we promise it will have a positive effect on your day.

Preparation

When getting up in the morning, get yoruself a pillow or a mat, choose a place where you feel calm and relaxed and sit on the floor crossed legs, make yoruself comfortable but try to keep your back straight. You can take your morning coffee first but it is better if it right after you wake up.

Steps

  • (optional) Put on your earphones with a music without lyrics, preferably something happy but not too catchy so that you can keep your motication. Or play our playlist on the right.
  • Take a deep breath and close your eyes gently and focus on the music you are listening or on the sounds around you
  • Inhale and exhale deeply for about 1 minute
  • Scan your body from the feet to the head and try to notice any sensations, ask your body "how are you?". And if you feel any tension anywhere just focus your breath on that point when you inhale
  • Let your mind wander, we all have a lot of toughts and things to remember on a daily basis, let all of the ones that are already there come up in your mind without jugement without stressing about them. Let those thought pass in your head like clouds moves in the sky, you are just an observer of those thoughs even the negative ones.
  • While your mind is wandering, you might lose focus and start being stressed and judgemental about your thoughts again. A simple way to keep that from happening is direct the focus on your breath, keep breathing seeply at a constant rate. When you focus on it, your heart will slow down, you mind will quiet down and your body starts to relax
  • when you feel like you are done and your mind feels relaxed, or the time you have set is over. Open your eyes gently while focusing on your breath, make it come back to normal slowly and then strech your body a little bit.
  • You are now ready for the day with a foucsed mind and a relaxed body, there is no better combination

Explanation

This session is taking place right after work to release all the stress of the day and reconnect with yourself after all this anxiety and stress. You can do it when you arrive at home or even at the office before leaving. It will also take around 5 minutes.

Preparation

This time it all on depends on the place you are, either at work, in your car, at home, in the school bus, in school,... You need to sit comfortably and this time again put some music if you want to.

Steps

  • (optional) Put on your earphones with a music without lyrics, preferably something happy but not too catchy so that you can keep your notification. Or play our playlist on the right.
  • Sit down comfortably and close your eyes
  • Breath deeply, feel the movement of your chest as you inhale and exhale. Move your head, up and down, left and right as you breath to relax your neck muscles that keep the tension of your stress of the entire day.
  • You can stop moving your head.Now breath in for 4 seconds and breath out for 4 seconds, do this 5 times.
  • If you lose focus, it is not a problem, acknowledge it and come back to the exercise, it will even improve your focus
  • Now think about all the stress you have experienced during the day, all the problems and frustrations that you have experienced, imagine it as a ball in your stomach that makes you feel uncomfortable. With every exhales the ball loses weight and size.
  • You breath in energy and breath out all the unresolved problems. You breath in joy and breath out stress.
  • The ball is not in your stomach anymore, and you feel free. Now stretch your arms up above your head and open your fingers, push as far as possible, move your shoulder back and forth.
  • Open your eyes and come back to a natural breath rate
  • You have successfully transitioned from work to home and you are ready to enjoy your free time at home

Explanation

And finally the last session of the day, right before sleeping after watching the TV, showering,... You are refreshed and this meditation is to prepare you for a high quality sleep. The goal is to let go everything painful or joyful, and disconnect with the world and get into a completely relaxed mindfulness. This session will be a around 10 min and will help improve the quality of your sleep.

Preparation

Find some peaceful place next to your bed where you can comfortably sit down on the floor, preferably on a pillow or a mattress to not hurt your back. Make sure that you do not have anything to do anymore apart from going to bed

Steps

  • (optional) Put on your earphones with a music without lyrics, preferably something happy but not too catchy so that you can keep your notification. Or play our playlist on the right.
  • Sit down on the floor with your back straight and start breathing deeply
  • When you feel like your body and your breathing have synchronized, scan your mind and let all your thoughts come up
  • Visualize yourself sitting beside a gently floating stream, next to you there is a big tree full of leaves and those leaves are all your thoughts. Put as many details possible, from the colors to the presence of fishes in the water.
  • Now imagine that each leaf falls on the lake, assign a thought to that leaf either joyful, painful or neutral and watch them float down the river. Even the "I don't have a thought" though should go on a leaf
  • When you realize that you don't have any thoughts left to put on the leaf, watch the stream flow at its own pace, calm and peaceful.
  • Notice the urge to rush the flow and resist it, you don't need to rush any thoughts or get ride of them. Accept them as they come and go.
  • If a thought comes by multiple times, watch it float as many times as you need to, any thought you are having even about the exercise, place on a leaf and let it go
  • Now that all the leaves have fallen from the tree and they have gone with the stream, watch the empty stream and focus on its pace
  • Gently return your awareness and prepare yourself to go to sleep without losing that freedom of mind, do not let thoughts come back, they have float down the stream and have left.
