SOMETIMES A WORKOUT IS ALL THE THERAPY YOU NEED

The relation between your mind and your body

It might be counter intuitive to think of a direct relation between your physical and mental well-being, but working out can actually reduce the risk of developing mental illness. It also help improving self-esteem, improving memory, helps you sleep better, and boosts your overall mood. When doing some exercise, the brain releases chemicals that help improve the mood which help a lot for depression and anxiety. Exercise is a powerful depression fighter for several reasons. Most importantly, it promotes all kinds of changes in the brain, including neural growth, reduced inflammation, and new activity patterns that promote feelings of calm and well-being. It also releases endorphins, powerful chemicals in your brain that energize your spirits and make you feel good. Finally, exercise can also serve as a distraction, allowing you to find some quiet time to break out of the cycle of negative thoughts that feed depression.

Running

If you are the kind of person that likes running outdoor, you prefer cardio to any other type of exercise. Cardio is very good for your heart and keeps you healthy, it has been said that running can heal different mental illnesses. Remember the goal is to build a habit with consistency not too get injured. This little schedule is for you.

Running is a fantastic activity to reduce stress, build endurance and be fit. You do not have to start with 10km, actually focusing on the time instead of a distance is more effective. At the beginning you could start with as little as 15minutes per day, and you can increase gradually by 5 minutes as you feel like it's getting easier. The best for you would be to run more than 40 minutes per day.

Yoga

More of a fan of physical postures, breathing techniques and a more relaxed type of sport. Yoga is the one for you, a practice of the mind and the body that promotes relaxation and flexibility. It will improve your breathing, your posture, your mood, your awareness and even your strength. This one is for you.

Yoga is a relaxing sport, it requires to focus on your body's movements and push it into doing positions that requires flexibility. You should practice every morning and night with the mediation, associating yoga and mediation is the best combination for a harmony between the mind and the body. Flexibility is quite hard to acquire and quite painful if you force too much, start slowly.

The importance of sleep

Sleep is as important to our bodies as eating and breathing. Sleeping well is essential to your wellbeing, and getting back to a well organized sleep schedule could be the first step of healing from mental problems. Do not listen to people telling you to get out of bed and pull yourself together, disturb sleep is a part of mental illness and in most cases it worsens it. Often changes in your daily life habits are enough to improve the quality of your sleep, that is why we want to introduce you to some simple relaxation exercises that can also help you for your sleeping problems.

Sleep needed per age range

School age (6-13 years old): 9-11 hours

Teen (14-17 years old): 8-10 hours

Young adult (18-25 years old): 7-9 hours

Adult (26-64 years old): 7-9 hours
